TIR & Dispersion of Light

An interactive 3D Unity XR simulation demonstrating Total Internal Reflection and the dispersion of white light into the visible spectrum — built for immersive physics education.

C# Unity Engine Unity XR Optics

Developed an interactive XR simulation that visualises two fundamental optical phenomena — Total Internal Reflection (TIR) and the Dispersion of Light. Using Unity XR Toolkit and real-time ray rendering, users can manipulate light sources, mediums, and prisms in a fully immersive 3D environment to observe how light behaves at boundaries between materials.

The simulation provides a hands-on environment for understanding the physics behind optical fibres, diamonds, rainbows, and prismatic effects — making abstract concepts tangible through direct interaction with VR controllers.

Total Internal Reflection (TIR)

TIR occurs when a ray of light travelling from a denser medium (e.g. glass or water) to a less dense medium (e.g. air) strikes the interface at an angle greater than the critical angle. Instead of refracting into the second medium, the entire ray is reflected back — resulting in perfect, lossless reflection.

  • Critical angle determined by Snell's Law: θc = arcsin(n₂ / n₁), where n₁ > n₂
  • Below the critical angle — light partially refracts and partially reflects
  • At exactly the critical angle — refracted ray travels along the boundary (90°)
  • Above the critical angle — no refraction; 100% of light is internally reflected
  • Real-world applications: optical fibres, endoscopes, diamonds, periscopes, binoculars
  • Simulation lets users adjust the angle of incidence and medium pair to observe TIR live

Dispersion of Light

Dispersion occurs because the refractive index of a medium varies with the wavelength of light. When white light passes through a prism, each colour (wavelength) bends by a different amount — separating into the visible spectrum (ROYGBIV). This is the same phenomenon responsible for rainbows.

  • Violet light (shorter wavelength) bends most; red light (longer wavelength) bends least
  • Governed by Cauchy's equation: n(λ) = A + B/λ²
  • White light splits into: Red → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Indigo → Violet
  • Angle of deviation depends on the prism apex angle and refractive index of glass
  • Natural examples: rainbows (water droplets act as prisms), oil-film iridescence, soap bubbles
  • Simulation renders each wavelength as a separate ray with accurate colour and curvature

Key Features

  • Real-time ray rendering for TIR and dispersion — angle changes update instantly
  • Adjustable angle of incidence with live critical-angle threshold indicator
  • Switchable medium pairs (glass-air, water-air, diamond-air) with different critical angles
  • Prism mode: white beam splits into full ROYGBIV spectrum with accurate deviation angles
  • XR Grab Interactables — rotate and move the prism or medium block with VR controllers
  • Informational tooltips explaining the physics at each interaction point
  • Designed as an educational tool for optics and physics courses
